History

Phil Knight, one of the two founders of Nike,
founded "Blue Ribbon Sports" in 1964,
which received the franchise to market the sports shoes of the Japanese company Onitsuka.
His founding partner was Bill Bowerman,
Knight's running coach at the University of Oregon. In the first year of operation,
the company sold 1,300 pairs of shoes and brought in about $8,000,
most of the sales were made out of the trunk of Nate's car.
In 1965 the company hired one full-time employee and the company's sales rose to $20,000.
In 1966, the company opened its first store in Santa Monica, California. br In 1969, sales reached a decent volume and Knight resigned from his position
as a lecturer at the University of Oregon and devoted himself to his work at the company full time.

The Logo Swoosh

The famous Nike logo was created in 1971 by Carolyn Davidson,
a graphic design student at the University of Portland.
Phil Knight, the founder of Nike,
met her when he was an accounting lecturer and hired her to do freelance work for his shoe company.
When Knight decided to create a new brand for his sneakers,
he approached Davidson and asked her to design several ideas.
She agreed, for a wage of two dollars an hour.
In June 1971, Davidson presented several design options to Knight and other company executives,
and the symbol known as the "Swoosh" was selected.
Davidson submitted an invoice for $35 for her work.
In 1983, as a gesture of gratitude, Knight gave Davidson a diamond ring with the symbol on it, as well as Nike shares.

Contrary to popular opinion,
the shape of the swoosh is not based on the famous statue of Nika
the Victorious (in the Louvre Museum).
The logo was designed a few months before the name Nike was adopted in August 1971 as the brand's new name.
The symbol was initially called simply "the strip" and only after some time received its familiar name.
